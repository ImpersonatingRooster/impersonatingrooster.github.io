PORTFOLIO

In 2017 I finally took the leap and dove into the Hobby of FPV drones. At the time, it was a relatively small hobby, and next to no one in the province was into it. So, I had no choice but to teach myself how to build and fly these drones using the online resources I could find online. I have always been attracted to things that had a steep learning curve, as the process of learning and hopefully eventually mastering said thing is one of the most satisfying things to me.

By the time I returned from my Backpacking trip in 2019, I had spent a large amount of time behind the goggles and had gotten pretty good at flying FPV drones. Throughout these two years, I was consistently posting on my drone instagram page. It was mostly acrobatic flying, but I would occasionally upload a cinematic FPV clip here and there, which had a really cool unique look compared to your regular drone shots. This got the attention of a small video production company, Picnic Studios. I worked there not only as a drone pilot, but as a camera operator, I handled sound recording, lighting, editing, and various other things from start to finish to complete a video project for a client. I was at Picnic for about 4 years, until I went to Dalhousie University for my Bachelor of Computer Science.
